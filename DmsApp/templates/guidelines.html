Disaster Safety Guidelines

Earthquakes

  • Drop, cover, and hold on: Drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy table or desk, and hold on until the shaking stops.
  • Stay away from windows and heavy furniture: Glass and objects can break and fall during an earthquake.
  • Evacuate tall buildings and structures: After the shaking stops, evacuate tall buildings and structures as soon as it is safe to do so.
  • Prepare an emergency kit: Include food, water, first-aid supplies, and other essential items.

Hurricanes and Tropical Storms

  • Evacuate if advised: If you are in a coastal area and authorities issue an evacuation order, leave as soon as possible.
  • Secure your property: Take steps to protect your property from potential damage.
  • Have a disaster supply kit: Include food, water, first-aid supplies, a battery-powered radio, flashlights, and other essentials.
  • Stay informed: Keep up with the latest developments and any new warnings or evacuation orders.

Tornadoes

  • Take cover: If indoors, go to a basement, storm cellar, or an interior room on the lowest floor.
  • Stay away from windows: Avoid windows, as they can break during a tornado.
  • Protect your head: Cover your head with your hands or a pillow to protect yourself from falling debris.
  • Evacuate mobile homes: Mobile homes are not safe during tornadoes; seek shelter in a nearby building.
